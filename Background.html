Purpose

The Express Highway Performance Comparator application compares roadway conditions on expressways in the Boston region between the years 2012 and 2015. The roadway conditions are reported in the form of performance measurement data. The performance measures used in this application include Average Travel Speed, Speed Index, Congested Time, Delay per Mile, and Travel Time Index.

Definitions of Performance Measures

Average Travel Speed is associated with specific roadway Traffic Message Channels (TMCs) and is calculated using travel times and segment lengths. The average observed travel speed is a good indicator of lack of mobility in a roadway network and is used for determining solutions to mobility problems.

  • Average Travel Speed = Total speed value for all data samples / total number of data samples

    • Speed Index indicates congestion more accurately than travel speed alone because low travel speed may be a result of low speed limits on certain facilities.

  • Speed Index = average speed / posted speed limit (of a TMC)

    • Congested Time (measured in minutes per peak-period hour) is the average number of minutes that drivers experience congested conditions during the peak period. Congestion is considered to persist when the average speed is less than 35 miles per hour (mph) on a limited-access roadway.

  • Congested Time = (number of samples with congestion below speed threshold / number of samples used) x number of minutes in measured period

    • Delay per Mile (displayed in seconds per mile) shows the extra time needed to traverse a TMC or corridor. This performance measure is presented in a per-mile metric to emphasize the intensity of congestion.

  • Delay per Mile = (average travel time - free flow travel time) / segment length

    • Travel Time Index directly compares peak-period travel time conditions with free-flow travel time conditions. Travel Time Index indicates how much contingency time should be considered to ensure an on-time arrival during the peak period versus optimum travel times.

  • Travel Time Index = average peak-period travel time / free-flow travel time
